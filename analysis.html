Full Analysis

Comprehensive demographic, affordability, and geographic findings from 849 first-time homebuyer transactions

Buyer Demographics

First-time homebuyers in El Paso are predominantly young professionals and growing families aged 25–44, with 50% concentrated in the 25–34 age bracket—prime household formation years.

Finding: The 25–34 age bracket represents exactly 50% of all first-time buyers. Income concentration is in the $50k–$150k range (75.1% of buyers), with median income at $97k. Only 7.2% earn below $50k, indicating limited down payment savings across the cohort.

Geographic Origin: Local vs. In-Migration

Understanding buyer origin reveals the primary drivers of demand and market pressure. Nearly three-quarters of first-time buyers originate from within Texas, with the vast majority staying in El Paso specifically.

Policy Implication: El Paso's first-time homebuyer demand is primarily endogenous—driven by local household formation and young adults aging into homeownership. Out-of-state migration, while present at 19.6%, is secondary to local household formation. This suggests market strategies should focus on expanding supply for local cohorts rather than controlling external demand.

Affordability: Price-to-Income Analysis

The price-to-income (P/I) ratio is a standard affordability metric. Values below 3x are typically considered reasonable entry-point pricing. El Paso's local cohort achieves 2.75x, but regional in-movers face 3.09x—indicating elevated affordability stress.

Housing Product Characteristics

The median first-time buyer home is 4 bedrooms, 2 bathrooms, approximately 1,927 square feet. This represents a notable shift from older central urban stock (3 bed, 1 bath, 1,628 sqft).

The preference for 4-bed/2-bath units reflects family formation patterns. Many first-time buyers are young parents or anticipate children. The shift from older 3-bed/1-bath urban units to suburban 4-bed/2-bath homes reflects broader suburban migration patterns and the concentration of new construction in the 79928, 79938, and 79934 zip codes.

Mortgage Product by Price Tier

Mortgage product selection varies dramatically by purchase price. Buyers in the under-$200k range rely overwhelmingly on FHA (55.0%) and VA (21.2%). Those buying in the $300k+ tier shift toward conventional loans (35.5%) and maintain strong VA presence (34.8%). This pattern reflects both buyers' financial capacity and the distribution of affordable entry-point inventory.

Destination Patterns by Buyer Origin

Geographic clustering of buyer origins by price tier reveals clear market segmentation.

Key Observation: Out-of-state buyers concentrate in the $200k–$300k price tiers (32.3% in the $250k-$300k range), suggesting they arrive with sufficient capital to access mid-market suburban new construction. Local buyers show a much flatter distribution across all price tiers. Out-of-state movers may be relocating with equity from prior home sales or job offers from employers offering relocation packages.

Policy Conclusions & Recommendations

1. El Paso's Market is Locally Driven

Nearly three-quarters of first-time buyer activity is from in-state and local sources. The narrative of out-of-state demand inflating local prices does not strongly support this data. Instead, policy should focus on expanding supply and affordability for endogenous household formation.

2. Affordability is Manageable but Tightening

The 2.75x price-to-income ratio for local buyers is reasonable, but regional in-movers face 3.09x ratios. Without supply expansion, affordability stress will increase. The dominance of FHA (43.6%) and VA (33.7%) indicates limited savings capacity across the cohort.

3. Demand is Highly Concentrated Geographically

Nearly 60% of first-time buyers purchase in just three zip codes (79928, 79938, 79934). This concentration creates supply scarcity and inflates prices relative to broader El Paso. Policy could explore incentives to expand inventory in underutilized areas.

4. Product Type and Price Are Tightly Linked

The dominance of FHA in lower-price tiers ($150k–$200k) versus conventional in upper tiers ($300k+) suggests that entry-point affordability is the critical policy lever. Expanding down-payment assistance programs, shared equity models, and community land trusts could unlock buyer cohorts unable to access FHA financing.

5. Military Population is a Stabilizing Force

VA-backed loans represent one-third of all first-time purchases. El Paso's military presence is a substantial advantage for household formation and provides a policy anchor. Military family relocation, base stability, and consistent income support broader demand.

Bottom Line: El Paso's first-time homebuyer market is resilient, locally rooted, and driven by household formation rather than external migration. The challenge is not demand, but supply: expanding inventory in high-demand corridors (79928, 79938, 79934) and broadening access to down-payment capital for income-constrained local buyers.

Methodology & Data Notes

Sample Definition

849 first-time homebuyer transactions (2025). Primary home purchase loans where the borrower is aged 25-44. Includes FHA, VA, conventional, and USDA loan types. Excludes refinances, home-equity loans, and non-primary purchases.

Data Sources

  • HMDA (Home Mortgage Disclosure Act): Loan terms, demographics, loan purposes, and underwriting outcomes
  • MLS (Multiple Listing Service): Property characteristics, pricing, and location
  • Data Axle: Historical mover tracking and prior geographic locations

Geographic Scope

El Paso County, Texas. Analysis focuses on specific zip codes and regional categories (Texas vs. regional adjacent states vs. long-distance out-of-state).

Important Notes

Income figures in HMDA are reported in thousands; all figures have been scaled to face values. Price-to-income ratios use stated household income and median purchase price at point of sale. This analysis is for informational purposes; figures may be subject to data processing and rounding.
